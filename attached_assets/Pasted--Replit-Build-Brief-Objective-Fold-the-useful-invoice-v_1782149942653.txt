### Replit Build Brief

**Objective**
Fold the useful invoice-viewing behavior into the main Accounts area, remove the separate flawed Customer Center, and rename the Accounts tab to **Customer Center**.

**User-visible problem**
The current app has both **Accounts** and **Customer Center**, which overlap. The existing Customer Center is unreliable, including invoices assigned to the wrong doctors. The better direction is to make the Accounts table become the true Customer Center, with practice/doctor selection and invoice viewing from that screen.

**Requested behavior**
On the current Accounts page:

* Add a checkbox next to each **practice** row, not just individual doctor rows.
* Keep checkboxes next to individual doctors.
* Add a top action: **View invoices for selected**.
* If the user selects a practice, the action should show all invoices tied to that practice.
* If the user selects one or more doctors, the action should show invoices tied to those doctors.
* The invoice view should support invoice statuses: **open**, **closed**, and **overdue**.
* Remove the existing separate **Customer Center** section/page from the Financial navigation.
* Rename the left-nav **Accounts** tab to **Customer Center**.
* The renamed Customer Center should use the current Accounts-style practice/doctor table as the source of truth.

**Exact parts of the app/code to inspect**

**Implementation plan**

1. Rename the visible sidebar label **Accounts** to **Customer Center**.
2. Keep the existing Accounts route if needed for compatibility, but update labels and page heading to Customer Center.
3. Remove the old Customer Center nav item under Financial.
4. Disable or redirect the old Customer Center route so users do not land on the flawed invoice assignment screen.
5. Add a checkbox to every practice row in the Accounts table.
6. Preserve doctor-level checkboxes under expanded practices.
7. Add selected-state handling that supports mixed selections:

   * selected practice IDs
   * selected doctor/provider IDs
8. Add a top toolbar button: **View invoices for selected**.
9. Disable the button until at least one practice or doctor is selected.
10. When clicked, open or navigate to an invoice list filtered by the selected practice/doctor IDs.
11. The invoice list must include filters/tabs for **Open**, **Closed**, **Overdue**, and ideally **All**.
12. Make invoice lookup use the actual selected practice/doctor relationships, not the old Customer Center assignment logic.
13. Add regression checks so invoices do not appear under the wrong doctor.

**Important constraints / guardrails**

* Do not reuse the old Customer Center invoice assignment logic if it is known to assign invoices to the wrong doctors.
* Selection should be clear and predictable:

  * Selecting a practice means “include invoices for this whole practice.”
  * Selecting an individual doctor means “include invoices for this doctor only.”
* If a practice and one of its doctors are both selected, avoid duplicate invoices.
* Do not show invoices from unrelated practices or unrelated doctors.
* The invoice query should filter by stable IDs, not display names.
* Overdue should be calculated from due date plus unpaid/open balance, not just invoice status label.
* Closed invoices should include paid/closed invoices even if open balance is zero.

**Acceptance criteria**

* The old Financial → Customer Center nav item is gone.
* The old flawed Customer Center page is no longer accessible from normal navigation.
* The existing Accounts tab is renamed **Customer Center**.
* Practice rows have checkboxes.
* Doctor rows still have checkboxes.
* A **View invoices for selected** action appears at the top of the table.
* Selecting one practice and clicking the action shows invoices for that practice.
* Selecting one doctor and clicking the action shows invoices for that doctor.
* Selecting multiple practices/doctors shows a combined, deduplicated invoice list.
* The invoice view can show open, closed, and overdue invoices for the current selection.
* Invoices are not assigned to or displayed under the wrong doctors.

**Regression risks to check**

* Existing invoices page should still work.
* Existing account/practice search should still work.
* Expanding/collapsing practice rows should not clear selections unexpectedly.
* Archived practices should follow the existing archived visibility rules.
* Invoice balances and open balances should match the Financial → Invoices source of truth.
* Any old Customer Center links/bookmarks should redirect safely to the new Customer Center or show a not-found page.

### Debug Analysis

**Probable root cause**
The app has two overlapping customer/account concepts: the Accounts table and the Financial Customer Center. The old Customer Center appears to have its own invoice grouping or assignment logic, which is producing incorrect doctor associations. The safer fix is to retire that surface and build invoice viewing from the Accounts table, where practice/doctor hierarchy is already clearer.

**Supporting evidence from screenshots**
The Accounts page already lists practices with expandable doctor rows and doctor-level checkboxes. The old Customer Center separately lists practices and invoices, includes a “Reassign all invoices...” action, and appears to be doing its own customer-to-invoice mapping. That separate mapping is the risky part.

**Alternate hypotheses worth checking**

* The invoice assignment bug may be in a shared invoice query used by both Customer Center and Invoices.
* The old Customer Center may be grouping invoices by practice name instead of provider/practice IDs.
* Some invoices may be linked to cases, while others may be manually created and linked differently.
* Overdue may currently be calculated inconsistently across invoice screens.

**Why the recommended approach is best**
It removes the duplicate flawed workflow and makes the practice/doctor hierarchy the single place where users choose whose invoices they want to see. That gives users a cleaner mental model and reduces the chance of invoices being shown under the wrong doctor.
