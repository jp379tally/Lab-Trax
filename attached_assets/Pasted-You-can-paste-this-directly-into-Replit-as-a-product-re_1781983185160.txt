# LabTrax AI Assistant Vision & Product Specification

## Overview

The LabTrax AI Assistant will be a deeply integrated, proactive artificial intelligence system embedded throughout the LabTrax platform. Unlike a traditional chatbot that only responds when asked, the LabTrax AI Assistant will actively assist users, learn from workflow patterns, understand dental laboratory terminology, provide intelligent recommendations, identify potential issues, and help users operate more efficiently.

The goal is to create an AI assistant that becomes a knowledgeable virtual employee for every dental laboratory using LabTrax.

---

# Core Objectives

The AI Assistant should:

* Understand all LabTrax functionality and workflows.
* Possess extensive knowledge of dental laboratory operations.
* Understand dental terminology, materials, workflows, and restorations.
* Understand HIPAA compliance and healthcare data handling requirements.
* Learn customer-specific nomenclature and business practices.
* Provide proactive recommendations rather than waiting for questions.
* Help reduce errors and missed steps.
* Improve efficiency and user productivity.
* Continuously learn from user behavior and system activity.

---

# Knowledge Domains

## LabTrax Platform Knowledge

The AI should understand:

* Case management
* Prescription workflows
* Invoicing
* Scheduling
* Customer management
* Production workflows
* Status tracking
* Notifications
* Reporting
* Billing
* Mobile workflows
* User permissions
* Task management
* Audit logs
* AI Reader functionality
* Existing AI Chat functionality

The assistant should be capable of answering any LabTrax-related question in natural language.

Examples:

"How do I add a remake?"

"Why is this invoice showing unpaid?"

"Where do I update customer pricing?"

"What does this status mean?"

---

## Dental Laboratory Knowledge

The AI should possess expert-level knowledge of:

* Crown and bridge
* Dentures
* Partials
* Implants
* Surgical guides
* All-on-X restorations
* Digital workflows
* Milling processes
* Printing workflows
* Zirconia
* Emax
* PMMA
* PFM restorations
* Shade systems
* Occlusion concepts
* CAD/CAM workflows
* Scanner integrations
* Lab communication best practices

---

## HIPAA Compliance Knowledge

The AI must understand:

* Protected Health Information (PHI)
* HIPAA regulations
* Secure data handling
* Privacy requirements
* User permissions
* Data retention policies
* Compliance recommendations

The AI should actively identify potential compliance risks and notify users when appropriate.

---

# Proactive Intelligence System

## Smart Suggestions

The AI should analyze activity in real time and provide contextual suggestions.

Examples:

* Missing shade information
* Missing due dates
* Missing doctor instructions
* Potential pricing discrepancies
* Unusual material selections
* Incomplete prescriptions
* Missing attachments
* Missing approvals

Suggestions should appear as small, non-intrusive assistant bubbles.

Example:

"Case #1042 appears to be missing shade information. Would you like me to flag it for review?"

---

## Workflow Recommendations

The assistant should identify opportunities for improvement.

Examples:

* Recommend faster workflows
* Suggest automation opportunities
* Identify bottlenecks
* Recommend commonly used materials
* Suggest preferred vendors
* Identify overdue tasks

---

## Risk Detection

The AI should identify:

* Cases at risk of missing due dates
* Missing required information
* Unusual pricing activity
* Potential compliance issues
* Production bottlenecks
* Customer communication gaps

---

# Personalized Learning Engine

## Behavioral Learning

The assistant should learn:

* Frequently used terminology
* Customer naming conventions
* Preferred materials
* User workflow habits
* Lab-specific abbreviations
* Common restoration types
* Pricing patterns

Examples:

The AI learns that a particular laboratory uses:

"Zirc" = Zirconia

"AOX" = All-On-X

"Temp PMMA" = Temporary PMMA Restoration

The assistant automatically understands and utilizes these terms.

---

## Adaptive Intelligence

Over time the AI should become increasingly personalized.

The assistant should remember:

* User preferences
* Frequently performed actions
* Common customer requests
* Preferred workflows
* Historical decisions

The system should become smarter with continued usage.

---

# Conversational AI Assistant

Users should be able to ask questions naturally.

Examples:

"Show me all cases due tomorrow."

"Which doctors have the highest remake rate?"

"Why is this case delayed?"

"Which invoices are over 30 days past due?"

"How many implant cases did we complete this month?"

The AI should retrieve and analyze data before providing answers.

# Notification System

The assistant should utilize:

* In-app notifications
* Mobile push notifications
* Desktop notifications
* Assistant pop-up bubbles

Notification priorities:

## High Priority

* HIPAA concerns
* Missed due dates
* Critical production delays

## Medium Priority

* Missing case information
* Invoice issues
* Approval reminders

## Low Priority

* Workflow recommendations
* Efficiency suggestions
* General insights

---

# AI Memory Architecture

The assistant should learn from:

* Audit logs
* User actions
* Case history
* Chat history

* Workflow activity
* Notification interactions

All learning should be permission-aware and HIPAA compliant.
