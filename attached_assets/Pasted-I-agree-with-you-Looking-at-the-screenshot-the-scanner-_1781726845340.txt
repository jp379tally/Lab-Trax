I agree with you. Looking at the screenshot, the scanner is behaving like a **full-camera barcode detector**, not a **targeted barcode locator**.

Right now the scanner is reading any barcode visible anywhere in the camera frame. That's why you're getting inconsistent behavior when multiple barcodes are on the page.

### What should change

## 1. Restrict scanning to the guide box

The white square/rectangle should not just be visual.

The scanner should:

* Calculate the bounds of the guide box.
* Ignore any barcode whose bounding box is not fully inside that region.
* Only process barcodes that intersect the scan window.

Expected behavior:

```text
Inside square = eligible

Outside square = ignored
```

In your screenshot:

```text
0002 = ignore
0004 = scan
next barcode = ignore
```

depending on which barcode is centered inside the guide box.

---

## 2. Single-barcode mode

Current behavior appears to be:

```text
Detect all barcodes
Take first result
```

You want:

```text
Detect all barcodes
Filter to scan window
Choose closest barcode to center
Lock scanner
Process result
Wait for confirmation
Allow next scan
```

This prevents:

* Double scans
* Jumping between barcodes
* Wrong case selection

---

## 3. Scan lock / debounce

After a successful read:

```text
0004 scanned
```

scanner should immediately:

* Freeze detection for 1–2 seconds
* Highlight scanned barcode
* Vibrate once
* Show patient name
* Show case number

Example:

```text
✓ 0004
Park Brittle
Case 26-51
```

Then continue scanning only after:

```text
Locate
Next Scan
```

or timeout.

## 6. Manual barcode assignment compatibility

You also requested:

```text
0001
0002
0003
...
0500
```

assigned manually during intake.

The lookup must be:

```text
barcode_value -> case
```

not

```text
barcode_image -> case
```

Meaning:

```text
0001
```

typed manually on desktop

must equal

```text
0001
```

scanned on mobile.

The scanner should read the numeric value and query the case tied to that exact barcode.
